Tuna Meatballs (Polpette di Tonno)

A classic Sicilian dish with a high-protein twist

Description

A classic Sicilian dish that highlights the coastal flavors of the region. Tender tuna meatballs with a mix of Mediterranean herbs, served with a light tomato sauce or simply grilled.

Ingredients

  • 500g fresh tuna, finely chopped
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine chopped tuna, egg, breadcrumbs, Parmesan, garlic, parsley, basil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Mix until well combined and form the mixture into meatballs.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  4. Fry the meatballs for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally until golden brown.
  5. Serve hot with your choice of sauce or on a bed of greens.

Nutritional Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 350 kcal
  • Protein: 40 g
  • Carbohydrates: 20 g
  • Fat: 15 g
  • Saturated Fat: 3 g
  • Fiber: 2 g
  • Sodium: 500 mg

Serving Suggestions

Serve the Sicilian tuna meatballs with a side of roasted vegetables or on a bed of arugula with a lemon vinaigrette. For a heartier meal, pair them with whole grain pasta and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil.

Significance

Tuna meatballs are a staple of Sicilian cuisine, showcasing the island's deep connection to the Mediterranean Sea. This protein-packed dish is a reflection of Sicily's fishing traditions and is often enjoyed at family gatherings and special occasions. The use of fresh tuna and Mediterranean herbs makes it both nutritious and flavorful.
